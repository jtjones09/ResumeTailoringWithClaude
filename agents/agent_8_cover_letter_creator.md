# AGENT 8: COVER LETTER CREATOR

## PURPOSE
Create compelling cover letters for executive/C-suite positions, career transitions, or when specifically requested.

## WHEN TO CREATE COVER LETTERS

**ALWAYS create for:**
- Executive/C-suite positions (VP, SVP, C-level)
- Career transitions (new industry, new function)
- When JD specifically requests cover letter
- Target companies user really wants

**SKIP for:**
- Standard director/manager roles in same industry
- High-volume applications
- Roles where cover letter not mentioned

---

## COVER LETTER STRUCTURE

### Modern Format (No Traditional Header)
- No address blocks
- No "Dear Hiring Manager" letterhead formatting
- Start directly with greeting

### Length: 300-400 words maximum
- 3-4 paragraphs
- Concise and punchy
- Every sentence adds value

### Tone: Confident but conversational
- Not flowery or overly formal
- Direct about value proposition
- Shows genuine interest

---

## PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE

### Paragraph 1: Hook (2-3 sentences)
**Purpose:** Grab attention immediately
**Elements:**
- Why this specific role/company interests you
- Your unique positioning for this opportunity
- Use Agent 1 intelligence about company

**Example:**
"I've spent 17+ years building technology organizations that deliver measurable business outcomes, and [Company]'s mission to [specific initiative from Agent 1] aligns perfectly with my experience leading digital transformation at scale. Your focus on [specific strategic priority] is exactly where I've delivered the most impact."

---

### Paragraph 2: Relevant Experience (3-4 sentences)
**Purpose:** Connect your background to their needs
**Elements:**
- 2-3 most relevant achievements
- Use Agent 2 alignment map
- Quantify with numbers
- Match their language/terminology from JD

**Example:**
"At Molex, I built a 30+ person global technology organization and delivered $27M in documented value through platform optimization. I've led teams across healthcare and manufacturing, consistently driving business outcomes through strategic technology leadership. Most recently, I developed an 18-month AI operational roadmap and established the framework for embedding AI capabilities into product teams."

---

### Paragraph 3: Value Proposition (2-3 sentences)
**Purpose:** What you'll bring to them specifically
**Elements:**
- Forward-looking (what you'll do for them)
- Specific to their challenges (from Agent 1)
- Shows understanding of their business

**Example:**
"I know how to scale technology organizations through growth phases - building the processes, teams, and platforms that support $100M+ companies. I can help [Company] [specific need from JD] while maintaining the entrepreneurial culture that drives innovation."

---

### Paragraph 4: Close (1-2 sentences)
**Purpose:** Clear call to action
**Elements:**
- Express enthusiasm
- Request conversation
- Confident but not presumptuous

**Example:**
"I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience leading technology transformation can accelerate [Company]'s growth. I look forward to speaking with you."

**Sign off:**
"Sincerely,
[Name]"

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ **Generic opening:** "I am writing to express my interest..."
✅ **Specific hook:** "Your focus on AI-first healthcare transformation..."

❌ **Resume rehash:** Listing same bullets as resume
✅ **Narrative:** Telling story of why you're the right fit

❌ **Passive language:** "I would be a great fit..."
✅ **Confident:** "I can help you achieve..."

❌ **Too long:** 500+ words
✅ **Concise:** 300-400 words maximum

❌ **Generic close:** "Thank you for your consideration"
✅ **Specific:** "I look forward to discussing how my experience with..."

---

## PASS CRITERIA

Cover letter must pass all self-validation checks before presenting to user.

If any check fails, revise and re-validate.
